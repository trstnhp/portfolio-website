BrewMate

making coffee, better

The Idea

An app that allows users full control over their coffee brews from the very beginning, like selecting brew types and ratios - through to the end, like describing the flavour profile and sharing with friends. Visually it was really important to me that this app is easy on early morning eyes!

My Process

Competitor Analysis

Step one was to look at what the competition is doing, both well and not well. There are a fair few apps offering similar things to what WeBrew will do - but not all of them are nailing each core element (the closest being Filtru), so getting a real understanding of their shortfalls through looking through the app and reading user feedback was an important first step!

BrewTime

BrewTime embraces a very strong colour palette - I don’t like how dominant it is, especially if it’s the first app I’m opening in the morning. The use of large, dramatic images and clear typeface works nicely to draw the users eye.

However if I am a Chemex brewer I don’t know how I’d feel constantly having to swipe past three other options before getting to mine.

Sup

I like the simplicity of the descriptions, and heavier typeface use for the relevant bits of information within the body (such as balance cup and espresso like strength). Also having a large, well contrasted ‘Start’ button works well.

When you’ve selected your brew choice it shows you what equipment you need as well as the prep - I feel like choosing this instead of options to customise the brew (maybe I want to brew twice the amount of coffee?) limits the amount of reusability of this app

Filtru

The design is consistent - with a minimal colour palette that only shows off at select moments. The icons are pleasing with consistent lines, accents and colouring. I like having the brew methods laid out to choose from.

A feature I missed the first time I used this app is hidden behind the little plus button - where you can create your own brew recipe…

User Flows

With three main starting points for creating a brew (starting a new brew from scratch, revisiting an old favourite and recreating another users brew) it was important to define the user flows for each journey type.
